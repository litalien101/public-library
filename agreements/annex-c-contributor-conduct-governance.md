# ANNEX C  
**Contributor Conduct & Governance Policy**  
Guardian Override — Version 1.0  

**Binding Legal Agreement — Community Conduct, Governance, and Enforcement Framework**

---

## C.0 Purpose

- **C.0.1** This Annex establishes Guardian Override’s Contributor Conduct & Governance Policy.  
- **C.0.2 Purpose:**  
  - Protect the integrity, safety, and professionalism of all Guardian Override projects.  
  - Define clear conduct expectations for all contributors.  
  - Set forth governance processes and community participation rules.  
  - Establish a transparent, auditable enforcement and appeals framework.  
  - Balance founder authority with contributor empowerment through transparent checks.  
- **C.0.3 Incorporation Clause:** This Annex is subordinate to the Master Enforcement Index (MEI v1.0) and the Contributor Agreement; MEI hierarchy and rules prevail in case of conflict.  
- **C.0.4 Updates:** Shall be versioned, published transparently, archived immutably, and communicated promptly to active contributors (any contributor with activity in the past 12 months).  

---

## C.1 Definitions

- **C.1.1 Contributor** — Any individual or entity submitting code, documentation, design, creative work, governance input, or community participation.  
- **C.1.2 Board** — Guardian Override Board of Directors or authorized delegate empowered to make binding decisions. Decisions are subject to Independent Panel review.  
- **C.1.3 Conduct Violation** — Any action breaching this Annex, MEI v1.2, or other Guardian Override policies. Annex conflicts resolved per MEI §1 hierarchy with Board/Governance Team guidance.  
- **C.1.4 Violation Taxonomy Mapping:**  
  - Minor → MEI §1 (Informational + Low)  
  - Moderate → MEI §2 (Medium + High)  
  - Major → MEI §3  
  - Critical → MEI §4  

### C.1.5 Minor Violation  
Low‑impact/unintentional conduct.  

**Thresholds:**  
- Advisory Notice: first‑time minor violation  
- Warning: ≥3 within 90 days  
- Escalation: ≥5 within 6 months → suspension  
- Reset: counts reset after 6 months without violations  

**Examples:**  
1. Forgetting to cite a source once → Advisory Notice  
2. Repeatedly ignoring formatting guidelines → Warning  
3. Failing to respond to review comments after multiple reminders → Warning  
4. Accumulating 5 warnings in 6 months → Suspension  
5. Submitting incomplete documentation repeatedly despite feedback → Warning  
6. Ignoring security best‑practice reminders in non‑critical code → Warning  

### C.1.6 Moderate Violation  
Repeated misconduct exceeding Minor thresholds but below Zero‑Tolerance.  

**Examples:**  
1. Consistently dismissive/obstructive behavior in governance discussions  
2. Repeated low‑quality/disruptive contributions despite guidance  
3. Persistent failure to comply with community standards short of harassment  
4. Failure to follow accessibility standards despite repeated reminders  

**Escalation Threshold:** ≥3 Moderate Violations in 12 months → discretionary Revocation review  

### C.1.7 Major Violation  
Serious misconduct with significant impact.  

**Examples:**  
1. Aggressive harassment  
2. Targeted defamation  
3. Sabotage of workstreams  
4. Unauthorized disclosure of nonpublic data  
5. Deletion or corruption of project assets  

### C.1.8 Critical Violation  
Severe misconduct threatening community integrity.  

**Examples:**  
1. Threats of violence  
2. Hate‑based harassment  
3. Coordinated attacks on contributors  
4. Severe data breaches  
5. Fraudulent identity or access attempts  
6. Repeated Major Violations after formal warnings  

- **C.1.9 Community Platform** — Any Guardian Override communication/collaboration space.  
- **C.1.10 Governance Team (GT)** — Maintains policy, monitors enforcement, manages appeals, oversees registry publication, resolves annex conflicts. Must recuse per MEI §11 and are subject to sanctions for misconduct.  
- **C.1.11 Independent Panel (IRP)** — Final appeal body; quorum ≥3, max 1 GT‑affiliated. Contributors may not serve.  
- **C.1.12 Contributor Representative** — Elected annually (simple majority, ≥25% participation, ≥6 months active contribution). Interim elections within 30 days if vacancy occurs.  
- **C.1.13 Advisory Committee** — Provides recommendations on conduct, licensing, recognition, governance.  
  - Term: 12 months renewable  
  - Quorum: ≥50% of appointed members  
  - Minority reports require ≥2 members  
  - Boundaries: may provide contextual insights; cannot enforce/adjudicate

## C.2 Contributor Conduct Standards

- **C.2.1 Respect** — Treat all participants with dignity, courtesy, professionalism.  
- **C.2.2 Integrity** — Submit only original/properly licensed work. No plagiarism, misrepresentation, or infringing material.  
- **C.2.3 Compliance** — Comply with Guardian Override policies, MEI, and applicable laws.  
- **C.2.4 Collaboration** — Constructive engagement in reviews, discussions, governance. No personal attacks.  
- **C.2.5 Safety** — Maintain an environment free from harassment, abuse, discrimination, exploitation, retaliation.  
  - Contributors must report violations; failure may itself be a conduct issue.  
  - Anonymous reporting permitted; named reporting may be required.  
  - GT must acknowledge reports within 7 days.  

### C.2.6 Zero‑Tolerance Violations (Critical Tier under Annex E)  
1. Hate speech/discriminatory slurs  
2. Doxxing or disclosure of personal info  
3. Threats, stalking, intimidation, targeted harassment  
4. Sexual harassment/solicitations/sexualized content  
5. Malicious sabotage of production/community systems  
6. Severe/repeated personal attacks  

- **C.2.7 Proportionality** — Minor accidental violations treated differently from willful misconduct; Moderate escalates proportionally; Zero‑Tolerance triggers Tier 3 enforcement.

## C.3 Governance Framework

- **C.3.1 Board Authority** — Final, binding. Published with rationale in Registry. IRP may override minor decisions.  
- **C.3.2 Community Input Channels** — Contributors may submit proposals via trackers, email, portals. Minority reports (≥2 dissenters) published alongside Board rationale.  
- **C.3.3 Advisory Committees** — Recommendations documented; cannot enforce/adjudicate.  
- **C.3.4 Transparency Reports (Quarterly):**  
  - **C.9.4(a) Tables** — Enforcement actions by category (Advisory, Warning, Suspension, Revocation).  
  - **C.9.4(b) Charts** — Monthly trends (≥12 months), stacked bar for repeated violations, optional pie charts; quarter‑over‑quarter comparisons required.  
  - **C.9.4(c) Narrative** — Trend commentary explaining shifts, systemic risks, governance updates.  
  - **Privacy:** pseudonymization, sensitive data redacted (MEI §9).  
  - **Publication:** PDF + Registry snapshot.

## C.4 Enforcement

- **C.4.1 Actions:**  
  a. Advisory Notice  
  b. Warning  
  c. Suspension (≤29 days for minor/moderate; longer per MEI §4)  
  d. Revocation  
  e. Legal Remedies  

- **C.4.2 Principles:**  
  - Severity, impact, intent, history, recurrence, community safety considered.  
  - Proportionality per GT; IRP substitutes if recused.  
  - Immediate action permitted for community safety.  
  - Actions logged, timestamped, archived per MEI §10: case logs 7y, consent 5y, systemic risk 3y.  
  - Contributors may request counsel/advisor support.  
  - Enforcement appealable once; new evidence may reopen review.  
  - Law enforcement: suspensions remain until external resolution.  
  - Notification within 7 days via Registry/portal.

## C.5 Appeals Process

- **C.5.1 Right to Appeal** — Any enforcement action above Advisory Notice may be appealed once.  
- **C.5.2 Submission Deadline** — Appeals must be submitted within 90 days; Major/Critical appeals: 14–30 days per MEI §6.  
- **C.5.3 Submission Requirements** — Written statement, supporting evidence, desired remedy.  
- **C.5.4 Recusal** — GT members directly involved must recuse.  
- **C.5.5 Review Authority** — GT handles standard appeals; IRP handles escalated/conflict appeals.  
- **C.5.6 Burden of Proof** — Preponderance of evidence; IRP applies “clear error” review.  
- **C.5.7 Timeline** — Initial review within 30 days; final determination within 60 days; expedited 14 days for Zero‑Tolerance cases.  
- **C.5.8 Suspension Pause Criteria** — Suspensions paused if prima facie evidence of misclassification, procedural error, or disproportionate severity.  
- **C.5.9 Remedies** — Uphold, modify, overturn, reinvestigate, or convert to restorative outcome.  
- **C.5.10 Finality** — Binding unless MEI emergency override invoked.  
- **C.5.11 Extraordinary Reopening** — Only for fraud, serious procedural error, or material new evidence.  
- **C.5.12 Publication** — Determinations published with violation severity, recurrence modifier, anonymized rationale, and redacted sensitive evidence.
