# ANNEX F  
**Moderation Playbook**  
Guardian Override — Version 1.0  

**Internal Guide for Moderators and Maintainers**

---

## F.0 Purpose
This Annex establishes a structured, enforceable playbook for Guardian Override moderators and maintainers. It defines responsibilities, principles, and workflows for managing community interactions, enforcing conduct standards, and supporting contributors. The Playbook ensures consistency, fairness, transparency, and accountability in all moderation practices, while safeguarding contributor wellbeing and organizational integrity.  

---

## F.1 Scope & Audience
Applies to all moderators, maintainers, Conduct Officers, and leadership engaged in community governance. It is binding across Guardian Override platforms and partner ecosystems.  

**Cross‑References:**  
- Annex D (Community Code of Conduct)  
- Annex E (Reporting Workflow & Enforcement Rubric)  
- Partner SOPs  
- Legal/Privacy Frameworks (GDPR, PIPA, etc.)  

---

## F.2 Objectives
- Provide moderators with clear, actionable guidance for daily enforcement.  
- Ensure enforcement decisions are proportional, documented, and appealable.  
- Integrate restorative practices alongside punitive measures to prioritize reintegration.  
- Establish measurable benchmarks for operational performance, wellbeing, and systemic risk detection.  
- Future‑proof protocols against emerging digital threats, hybrid/off‑platform incidents, and evolving governance needs.  
- Reinforce trust and transparency through structured reporting, audits, and community consultation.  

---

## F.3 Moderator Responsibilities
Moderators and maintainers are entrusted with the following responsibilities:  
- **F.3.1 Monitoring** — Continuously observe discussions, contributions, and community spaces for compliance with Annex D.  
- **F.3.2 Responding** — Intervene promptly in cases of misconduct; escalate if unresolved within 72 hours.  
- **F.3.3 Supporting** — Provide guidance, reassurance, and resources to contributors experiencing conflict or harm.  
- **F.3.4 Documenting** — Record incidents, interventions, and outcomes in secure logs within 24 hours.  
- **F.3.5 Escalating** — Refer serious, repeated, or unresolved cases to Conduct Officers or the Board within 7 days.  
- **F.3.6 Boundary Management** — Maintain professional boundaries; avoid bias or over‑involvement.  
- **F.3.7 Conflict‑of‑Interest Disclosure** — Recuse from cases where conflicts exist; notify Conduct Officers immediately.  
- **F.3.8 Training & Continuous Learning** — Participate in quarterly refreshers on conduct, platform updates, and best practices.  
- **F.3.9 Systemic Risk Flagging** — Identify and report recurring patterns or systemic risks.  
- **F.3.10 Periodic Reporting** — Provide quarterly summaries on incident trends and community health.  
- **F.3.11 Collaboration with Security Teams** — Coordinate on AI misuse, impersonation, phishing, or digital threats.  
- **F.3.12 Legal Liaison Role** — Coordinate with legal/advisory teams for critical incidents.  
- **F.3.13 Cross‑Platform SOP Integration** — Align enforcement with partner SOPs for hybrid/off‑platform cases.  
- **F.3.14 Consistency** — Apply rules uniformly across contributors and contexts.  
- **F.3.15 Wellbeing & Stress Monitoring** — Track stress levels, rotate duties every 4–6 hours, and debrief after Tier 3–4 incidents.  

---

## F.4 Moderation Principles
Moderation must be guided by:  
- **F.4.1 Fairness** — Apply rules impartially; safeguard via randomized case audits.  
- **F.4.2 Proportionality** — Match interventions to severity, intent, and impact; use Annex E proportionality matrix.  
- **F.4.3 Transparency** — Communicate decisions clearly; document rationale.  
- **F.4.4 Safety** — Prioritize wellbeing and community protection; Tier 3–4 cases trigger wellbeing review.  
- **F.4.5 Boundaries** — Respect personal limits; rotate duties during high‑volume periods.  
- **F.4.6 Confidentiality** — Protect sensitive information; store evidence securely.  
- **F.4.7 Non‑Retaliation** — Shield reporters and witnesses from reprisal.  
- **F.4.8 Cultural Sensitivity & Accessibility** — Respect diverse backgrounds; log accommodations.  
- **F.4.9 Restorative Practices** — Prioritize reintegration and constructive resolution over punitive measures.  

---

## F.5 Moderation Workflow
1. **Observe or Receive Report** — Identify misconduct directly or via reports.  
2. **Assess Severity** — Evaluate impact, intent, urgency using Annex E matrix.  
3. **Apply Decision Matrix** — Select enforcement pathway.  
4. **Intervene** — Apply measures (reminder, warning, restriction, suspension).  
5. **Document** — Record incident details and rationale.  
6. **Escalate** — Refer serious/repeated cases to Conduct Officers or Board.  
7. **Follow‑Up** — Communicate resolution; offer restorative pathways.  
8. **Reporter Notification** — Acknowledge moderator action within 48 hours.  
9. **Feedback Loop** — Moderators receive post‑resolution feedback.  
10. **Emergency Authority Limits** — Temporary interventions allowed; imminent harm escalated to Conduct Officers.  

---

## F.6 Communication Guidelines
Moderators must communicate with clarity, professionalism, and respect.  
- **F.6.1 Clarity** — Use simple, direct language.  
- **F.6.2 Neutrality** — Maintain calm, impartial tone.  
- **F.6.3 Respect** — Treat all contributors with dignity.  
- **F.6.4 Transparency** — Explain rules and rationale.  
- **F.6.5 Confidentiality** — Restrict sensitive details.  
- **F.6.6 Consistency** — Apply standardized language.  
- **F.6.7 Documentation** — Record communications in enforcement logs.  

---

## F.7 Common Scenarios
Tiered guidance for scenarios such as:  
- Heated arguments (Tier 1–2) → de‑escalation and reminders.  
- Persistent negativity (Tier 2–3) → warnings, restrictions if necessary.  
- Boundary violations (Tier 3–4) → immediate suspension, escalation.  
- Manipulation (Tier 3) → document evidence, escalate.  
- Security issues (Tier 3–4) → report vulnerabilities, restrict access.  
- Content policy violations (Tier 2–4) → warnings, restrictions, escalation.  
- Hybrid/off‑platform incidents (Tier 2–4) → coordinate across platforms.  
- Emerging digital threats (Tier 3–4) → address AI misuse, phishing, impersonation.

## F.8 Moderator Self‑Care
Principles:  
- Rotation of duties  
- Mandatory breaks  
- Stress monitoring and debriefs after Tier 3–4 incidents  
- Escalation of capacity limits  
- Peer support and access to wellbeing resources  
- Confidentiality in wellbeing reports  

---

## F.9 Appeals
- Appeals handled by Conduct Officers, leadership, or review panel.  
- Moderators’ role is documentation only.  
- Acknowledgment within 7 days; resolution within 30–60 days (max 90).  
- Cross‑border/high‑risk appeals resolved within 60 days.  
- Missed deadlines escalate to Board.  
- Transparency and confidentiality required.
