# ANNEX D  
**Community Code of Conduct**  
Guardian Override — Version 1.1  

**Behavioral Expectations and Enforcement Framework**

---

## D.1 Purpose

This Annex establishes Guardian Override’s Community Code of Conduct. Its purpose is to define behavioral standards, prohibited conduct, and enforcement mechanisms designed to protect the integrity, safety, and sustainability of all Guardian Override projects and community spaces.

**This Code applies to:**
- All Guardian Override platforms, repositories, governance processes, and community interactions.  
- Private communications between contributors (including direct messages, off‑platform contact, and social media) when such conduct has a direct, material impact on community safety or project integrity.  
- External events, conferences, and real‑world meetups organized under Guardian Override, in whole or in part, including partner‑hosted or hybrid events presented under the Guardian Override name.  

This Annex complements Annex C (Contributor Agreement) and operates under the authority of the Guardian Override Board.  

---

## D.2 Core Principles

All participants in Guardian Override spaces must uphold:  
- **Respect** — Treat all individuals with dignity, courtesy, and professionalism.  
- **Integrity** — Act honestly and transparently in contributions and interactions.  
- **Inclusivity** — Foster a welcoming environment that values diverse perspectives while prohibiting ideological conflicts based on political, religious, or conspiratorial debates. Constructive disagreement is encouraged when focused on ideas and expressed respectfully.  
- **Accountability** — Take responsibility for actions, decisions, and commitments.  
- **Safety** — Maintain a secure environment free from harassment, exploitation, or harm.  
- **Professional Boundaries** — Respect personal limits, avoid emotional labor demands, and maintain healthy, sustainable collaboration practices.  

---

## D.3 Expected Behaviors

Participants are expected to demonstrate:  
- **Constructive Communication** — Engage with clarity, empathy, and professionalism.  
- **Good‑Faith Collaboration** — Cooperate toward shared goals, assuming positive intent.  
- **Conflict Resolution** — Use designated channels, de‑escalate disputes, and avoid personal attacks.  
- **Respect for Privacy & Pseudonymity** — Honor disclosure choices and confidentiality.  
- **Adherence to Guidelines** — Follow Guardian Override governance policies and operational standards.  
- **Responsible Security Behavior** — Report vulnerabilities responsibly and in accordance with the Guardian Override Vulnerability Disclosure Policy.  
- **AI Usage Transparency** — Clearly attribute AI‑generated or AI‑assisted work, including tool name and version where relevant.  
- **Machine‑Translation Disclosure** — Disclose when content has been machine‑translated, except for non‑critical, casual messages.  

---

## D.4 Prohibited Behaviors

**Personal & Interpersonal Misconduct**
- Harassment or Discrimination — Any targeted conduct based on identity, background, or beliefs.  
- Abusive or Disruptive Conduct — Hostility, aggression, or intentional obstruction.  
- Dogpiling, Derailing, or Persistent Negativity — A documented pattern of disruptive or toxic behavior with observable community impact.  

**Integrity & Trust Violations**
- Misinformation or Disinformation — Knowingly spreading false or misleading information.  
- Manipulation or Dishonesty — Including fraud, misrepresentation, or deceptive practices.  
- Failure to Attribute AI‑Generated Work — Considered misconduct under this Code.  

**Security & Privacy Violations**
- Unauthorized Access or Exploitation — Any misuse or compromise of Guardian Override systems.  
- Boundary Violations — Including emotional labor demands, unsolicited DMs, pressure to disclose identity, or doxxing.  

**AI‑Related Misuse**
- Using AI tools to harass, impersonate, manipulate, or generate harmful content.  

**Abuse of Enforcement Systems**
- False, Malicious, or Retaliatory Reporting.  
- Coordinated External Harassment — Recruiting or incentivizing outside actors to target Guardian Override contributors.  

---

## D.4 Reporting & Enforcement

Guardian Override enforces this Code through a structured, proportional enforcement framework.  

**Reporting Channels**
- conduct@guardianoverride.org  
- legal@guardianoverride.org  
- Conduct Officers or designated governance portals  

**Conduct Officers**  
Appointed, vetted, and trained individuals with authority to:  
- Receive and investigate reports  
- Preserve evidence  
- Recommend sanctions  
- Liaise with the Guardian Override Board  

**Evidence Handling**  
- Minimum retention period: two years  
- Strict confidentiality and tamper‑prevention protocols apply  

**Confidential & Anonymous Reporting**  
- Anonymous reports are accepted, though they may limit possible remedies if evidence cannot be verified.  

**Non‑Retaliation**  
- Retaliation against good‑faith reporters is strictly prohibited.  

**Sanctions**  
Sanctions may be issued by Conduct Officers or the Board, including:  
- Warning — Formal notice  
- Mediation — Facilitated conflict resolution  
- Suspension — Temporary access removal  
- Ban / Revocation — Permanent removal for severe or repeated violations  
- External Escalation — Referral to applicable law enforcement or regulatory bodies for serious misconduct  

---

## D.5 Appeals Process

Contributors subject to enforcement actions may submit appeals under the following framework:  
- **(a) Submission Deadline** — Within 30 days of notification.  
- **(b) Review Authority** — The Board or a designated Appeals Committee.  
- **(c) Conflict‑Free Review** — Members must recuse themselves when conflicts exist.  
- **(d) Third‑Party Mediation** — At the Board’s discretion.  
- **(e) Repeat Offenders** — One appeal per enforcement decision.  
- **(f) Non‑Appealable Actions** — Automated anti‑spam or technical safeguard actions.  
- **(g) Finality** — Appeal decisions are final, binding, and issued with a written rationale.  
- **(h) Response Timeline** — 30 days for minor cases; 60 days for complex cases.  
- **(i) Enforcement Continuity** — Filing an appeal does not pause enforcement.  
- **(j) Public Disclosure** — Only updated if the enforcement action is overturned.  

---

## D.6 Scope

This Code applies to:  
- Guardian Override platforms, repositories, and governance processes  
- Social media use related to Guardian Override  
- Conferences, meetups, and real‑world events organized under Guardian Override  
- Communications with Guardian Override staff, contractors, and representatives  
- Off‑platform behavior that materially impacts community safety or project integrity  
- Partner or affiliate events presented under the Guardian Override brand  

**Limitation Clause**  
Guardian Override does not regulate private, unrelated aspects of contributors’ lives. Jurisdiction applies only where conduct materially affects community safety or project integrity.  

---

## D.7 Publication & Versioning

- **(a) Distribution** — This Code accompanies all distributed source code and documentation.  
- **(b) Versioning** — Uses semantic versioning (e.g., v1.0 → v1.1).  
- **(c) Notification** — Contributors will be notified within seven (7) days of publication.  
- **(d) Approval Authority** — Updates require Board approval.  
- **Authority hierarchy:** Board > Conduct Officers > Maintainers.  
- **(e) Archiving** — Archived versions stored in the governance repository in PDF and Markdown formats.  
- **(f) Binding Effect** — Continued participation constitutes acceptance. Contractual agreements supersede this Annex where conflicts arise.  
- **(g) Emergency Updates** — Security‑critical revisions may be published immediately.  
- **(h) Supersession** — New versions supersede prior versions unless otherwise stated.  

---

## D.8 Effective Date

Effective immediately upon publication.  
- **Transitional Grace Period:** 14 days for contributors to adapt to new policies.  
- **Retroactivity:** Past conduct may be reviewed only to address ongoing risks. Retroactive review is protective, not punitive.  

---

## D.9 Enforcement Transparency

Guardian Override will publish anonymized transparency reports annually to ensure accountability and trust.  

**Report Contents**  
- Number and types of violations  
- Enforcement actions taken  
- Appeals outcomes  
- Improvements to enforcement processes  

**Confidentiality & Privacy**  
- No identifying information included, unless required by law.  
- Additional anonymization is applied in small communities to prevent inference.  

**Publication**  
- Reports published publicly in Guardian Override repositories.  
- Annual release within 90 days of year‑end; interim reports permitted if warranted.  

---

## D.10 Community Roles & Responsibilities

### Moderators
- Enforce day‑to‑day conduct standards  
- Must complete conduct/governance training  
- Escalate significant violations to Conduct Officers  

### Maintainers
- Ensure project integrity  
- Apply governance policies consistently  
- Must complete conduct/governance training  
- Escalate violations to Conduct Officers  

### Conduct Officers
Must demonstrate:  
- Neutrality — Impartiality during investigations  
- Confidentiality — Protection of sensitive information  
- Conflict‑Free Operation — Mandatory recusal when necessary  
- Training Completion — Governance and investigative training  
- Evidence Preservation — Secure documentation and storage  

### Board
- Final authority over enforcement, appeals, and updates  
- Maintains governance oversight and ensures alignment with organizational mission
