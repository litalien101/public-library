# ANNEX E  
**Reporting Workflow & Enforcement Rubric**  
Guardian Override — Version 1.0  

**How Misconduct Is Reported, Reviewed, and Addressed**

---

## E.0 Purpose
- **E.0.1** Establishes Guardian Override’s standardized workflow and rubric for reporting misconduct, reviewing cases, and applying enforcement actions.  
- **E.0.2** Ensures fairness, transparency, and consistency across all Guardian Override platforms, events, and governance processes.  
- **E.0.3 Cross‑References:** Complements Annex C (Contributor Conduct & Governance Policy) and Annex D (Community Code of Conduct).  
- **E.0.4 Definition of Misconduct:** Includes violations of Annex D, Annex C, or governance policies, whether on‑platform, off‑platform, or hybrid/partner events.  
- **E.0.5 Definition of Material Impact:** Actions that meaningfully affect safety, functionality, governance, or reputation.  
- **E.0.6 Attempted Violations:** Includes attempted breaches, harassment, or manipulation, even if unsuccessful, if intent is proven.  

---

## E.1 Definitions
- **E.1.1 Preponderance of Evidence** — Misconduct more likely than not to have occurred.  
- **E.1.2 Confidentiality** — Obligation to protect sensitive information and identities.  
- **E.1.3 Appealable Action** — Any manual sanction issued under Levels 2–4.  
- **E.1.4 Witness** — Any individual providing testimony or evidence.  
- **E.1.5 Constructive Notice** — Indirect acknowledgment sufficient to confirm report submission.  
- **E.1.6 Emergency Action** — Immediate suspension/restriction to prevent imminent harm, reviewed within ≤72 hours.  
- **E.1.7 Imminent Harm** — Credible threats or actions likely to cause immediate danger.  
- **E.1.8 Imminent Threat vs. Potential Threat** — Immediate, credible danger vs. risks lacking urgency.  
- **E.1.9 Vulnerable Reporter** — Reporters requiring enhanced confidentiality safeguards (e.g., minors, marginalized groups).  

---

## E.2 Reporting Workflow
1. Submit a Report — conduct@guardianoverride.org, legal@guardianoverride.org, Conduct Officers, or governance portals. Anonymous reports accepted.  
2. Acknowledgment — Confirmation within ≤7 days. Constructive notice counts for anonymous reports.  
3. Initial Review — Conduct Officers complete review within ≤14 days.  
4. Emergency Action — Immediate suspension if credible risk exists. Reviewed within ≤72 hours.  
5. Investigation — Completed within ≤30 days; extensions ≤60 days with Board approval. Max duration ≤90 days.  
6. Conflict of Interest — Officers must recuse if conflicts exist.  
7. Determination — Findings reviewed by Conduct Officers, escalated to Board if necessary.  
8. Documentation — All stages logged securely.  
9. Communication — Decisions communicated in writing within ≤7 days.  
10. Appeal — Respondents may appeal under Annex C §5.  

---

## E.3 Enforcement Levels
- **Level 1** — Minor reminders, coaching, informal guidance.  
- **Level 2** — Formal warnings, temporary restrictions (7–180 days).  
- **Level 3** — Suspensions, revocation of contributor status.  
- **Level 4** — Permanent bans, legal escalation.  

**Escalation Rules:**  
- Repeated Level 1 → Level 2  
- Repeated Level 2 → Level 3  
- Single severe act → Level 3 or 4  
- Compound sanctions → escalate accordingly

## E.4 Enforcement Principles

- Notice of Allegations  
- Proportionality  
- Confidentiality & Witness Protection  
- Transparency to Respondents

- Anti‑Bias & Cultural Sensitivity  
- Accessibility accommodations  
- Least Necessary Action  
- Restorative Pathways  

---

## E.5 Recordkeeping
- Case logs ≥7 years  
- Consent logs ≥5 years  
- Systemic risk logs ≥3 years  
- Law enforcement referrals retained per jurisdiction +1 year  
- Secure deletion after retention unless ongoing risk  
- Encrypted, access‑controlled, audited annually  
- Transparency linkage: aggregated statistics feed into annual transparency reports  

---

## E.6 Special Protocols
- High‑Risk Cases → Immediate escalation to Board + law enforcement referral  
- Mandatory Reporting → Required for minors or threats of bodily harm  
- Frivolous Reports → Malicious reports may trigger sanctions under Annex D  
- Cross‑Border Handling → GDPR/PIPA compliance  
- Multi‑Reporter Cases → Consolidated investigation, confidentiality preserved  
- Internal Escalation Hierarchy → Prioritize imminent safety threats → security breaches → severe harassment  
- Conduct Officer SOP → Training, conflict disclosure, documentation, periodic review  

---

## E.7 Amendment & Review Cycle
- Annual Review by Board with stakeholder input  
- Emergency Amendments allowed with logged rationale  
- Community Consultation for drafts unless emergency  
- Approval Authority: Board  
- Publication in PDF + Markdown, semantic versioning  
- Notification within ≤7 days  
- Supersession: new versions override prior unless specified
